ALTMANN(I) was the first to point out the ventricular situation of the dividing cells and laid down the rule that in all organs of epithelial origin, cell division takes place on one surface only, that farthest removed from the mesoderm. HIS(2) confirmed this statement in his study of the human embryo of one month. RAUBER(3),on the contrary, denied any “seat of predilection” for the dividing cells.
